When you are young, you pass by many strangers without thinking about it. You always carry a friend; who was your friend? A teddy bear, a doll, an action figure, etc. It went everywhere with you, and while to everyone else, it was just a toy, to you it was a living being. Not in the same way we are alive; toys don't eat, drink or breathe to our knowledge. But they are alive in a way that allows you to create a separate world from our percieved one, called "reality", and you are the most important person there.

As you grow older, some people loose touch with their friend and their world. You go to school, you work, you worry excessively over things that involve surviving in our modern society. You become ill, unbalanced. We all look for a spark which will bring us back to that feeling we had as children, the feeling of escaping to somewhere new and unknown, but at the same time it is known by many of us. For some people, that escape is love, for others, it's traveling, perfecting a skill, driving on the open road, or something else. Then there are those of us who decide to travel back to childhood. This is often discouraged by other adults. They convince you that a toy is a tool that can only be used on children, only once in our lifetime and we can never return to it again in the same way.

I am a stranger on the internet who is telling you that this is not true. Ignore what everyone says. You can unlock that part of your mind again after leaving it for many years. Adults *can* play! And that is the message that I would like to get out to the world. There are many people who are now coming back to toys. Some have kept the same ones from childhood for years while others have gone out and searched for them and bought them again. As a toy collector, you are preserving an important part of not only your past but everyone else's past and their memories. When archaeologists unearth new things that show us how people lived, it's always an exciting discovery. Nobody says "That is boring", "That is uninteresting because it does not resemble the present". It's good to remember. Become your own archaeologist, and you will find that a wave of memories will come back to you as soon as you hold a toy in your hands again.

Thank you and I hope that you enjoy your experience!
